How Subsidies Strain our Economy

As Americans struggle to afford food, housing, and utilities for themselves and their families, their taxes continue to be stolen and used for programs that don't serve to help our society.

For example, it may surprise you that 24% of SNAP recipients are overweight, and 54% are obese.

Yet, hard-working taxpayers continue to pull money out of their pockets to support a failing program. More than a hundred billion dollars go to SNAP alone each year. The average American will pay $30,000 for the SNAP program in their lifetime. This money should be sent on college tuition and raising children, but instead it feeds people who don't need food.

I don't mean to say that we should completely get rid of supplemental nutrition programs. Safety nets are fine in moderation; the problem is that these programs are very often exploited and overused. Nutrition programs should be temporary, helping poor citizens get along while looking for a new job or way to support themselves.

However, many recipients take the program for granted, acting as if they are entitled to the fruit of other taxpayers' labor. A food stamp recipient on Tiktok says, "You're gonna tell me I don't deserve brownies...Get off your throne of entitlement."

No, assistance programs are not owed to anyone or by anyone. They are charity. As a result, we shouldn't be so demanding on taxpayers to support these programs; instead, churches and private charities should be the ones doing the heavy lifting for helping people get fed.
